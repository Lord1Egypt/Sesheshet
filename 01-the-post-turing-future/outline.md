**Arguments:** Turing's original 1950 paper was a philosophical provocation, not a rigorous test, The test rewards mimicry, not understanding — ELIZA passed by accident in 1966, Modern LLMs pass trivially, proving the test measures human gullibility, not machine intelligence, The test's death leaves a vacuum: we have no framework for what intelligence even means anymore
